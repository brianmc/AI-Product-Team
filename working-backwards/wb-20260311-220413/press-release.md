# High Flyer Gives Xfinite Cardholders a Reserved Seat at the Most Sought-After Bars in Cities They've Never Called Home

**Xfinite's affluent cardholders — urban socialites who command the room in their own city — can now walk into the most exclusive speakeasy bars in New York, Chicago, Miami, Los Angeles, and beyond with a table waiting, even when they're new in town.**

New York, NY — March 11, 2026 — Xfinite today launched High Flyer, a web application that gives Xfinite affluent cardholders priority access to reserved tables at the most coveted hidden bars across major US cities — at short notice, with no local connections required.

## The Problem

Xfinite cardholders are not tourists. In their home city, they know the owners, they know the staff, and they never wait. That social capital — built over years of showing up — is what gets them into the places that don't advertise, don't take OpenTable reservations, and often don't have a sign on the door.

When they travel, that capital doesn't transfer. They land in Austin or Seattle with one night free, wanting the kind of bar that a local regular would know — and they hit a wall. The city's best speakeasies and invite-only cocktail lounges are full, or appear to be. Without a personal introduction or a known face behind the bar, there is no table. Not tonight.

The result is a gap that no concierge fully closes: the concierge knows hotels and restaurants, not the unmarked door on the third floor of a warehouse that doesn't accept walk-ins. Cardholders end up at places that are merely good rather than the ones worth talking about.

## The Solution

High Flyer removes the access barrier entirely. The application presents Xfinite cardholders with a curated, city-specific selection of hidden and high-demand bars — the kind of venues that are difficult to book even with time and local knowledge. Cardholders choose a city, select a venue, and confirm a table for the same evening or the next. The reservation is guaranteed; no calling ahead, no name-dropping required.

The selection is deliberately small. High Flyer does not surface every bar in the city — it surfaces the ones that matter to this customer, vetted for exclusivity, quality, and the kind of scene a well-connected urban traveler would recognize as the real thing.

Cardholder status is the key. The access is built into the card benefit — not a points redemption, not an upgrade request. Cardholders open the app, pick the city they are in, and have a confirmed table.
