# Venues and Curation

High Flyer does not surface every bar in a city. It surfaces the ones that a well-connected urban traveler would consider worth the trip — the places that are difficult to access without a personal introduction or a known face at the door.

---

## How venues are selected

Every venue on High Flyer is assessed against a standard built around three criteria:

**Exclusivity.** The venue must be genuinely difficult to access without connections. That typically means no public booking systems, limited walk-in capacity, and a culture of regulars who treat the room as their own. A venue that is merely popular or has good reviews does not qualify.

**Quality.** The drinks, the environment, and the service must meet the standard a discerning urban socialite would recognize. The bar that the press described as one of the best in the country five years ago and then declined is not the same bar. High Flyer assesses the current experience, not the legacy reputation.

**Scene.** The venue must have the kind of atmosphere — and clientele — that a well-connected traveler would feel at home in. This is not about décor. It is about whether the room has the energy of a place people go to by choice, not convenience.

## What "curated" means in practice

The selection you see is deliberately small. On any given evening in a given city, you may see a handful of venues — not a scroll of options. That is intentional.

Every venue on the list has been vetted. Every venue has agreed to hold availability for Xfinite cardholders. The list you see is not a directory — it is a recommendation from someone who has been to every room on it.

---

## Ongoing quality control

Venues are not admitted to High Flyer permanently. The curation process is continuous:

- Venues that change ownership, decline in quality, or become too widely known to retain genuine exclusivity are removed from the program.

## Giving feedback on a venue

If a venue did not meet the standard — the experience was not what High Flyer's description led you to expect, the service was indifferent, or the room felt like the bar had lost what made it worth visiting — you can flag it.

## A note on venue discretion

Some venues on High Flyer prefer not to be publicly associated with card benefit programs. You may notice that venue names are partially presented, that addresses are given only as neighborhoods, or that the venue asks you not to discuss your High Flyer booking with other guests. Please respect these preferences. They are the terms under which these venues participate — and participation is what makes the product work.

---

## Venue availability over time

High Flyer inventory is not static. Venues may:

- Reduce their available slots on busy evenings
- Temporarily pause their participation during private events or renovations
- Exit the program

A venue you booked in a city on a previous trip may not be available on a subsequent visit. This is a feature of how genuine exclusivity works, not a system error. Check the current available list each time you visit a city.
